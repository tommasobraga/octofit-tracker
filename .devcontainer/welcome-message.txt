💡 Codespaces tips to avoid exceeding your monthly quota:
   • Always STOP your Codespace when not actively using it (do NOT just close the browser tab).
     → In GitHub.com go to: github.com/codespaces → find your codespace → ⋯ menu → "Stop codespace"
   • Use the smallest machine type (2-core) when creating a new Codespace.
   • Included monthly Codespaces quota (a 2-core machine uses 2 core-hours per hour of active use):
       GitHub Free plan:        120 core-hours/month (~60 hours on a 2-core machine)
       GitHub Copilot Pro plan: 180 core-hours/month (~90 hours on a 2-core machine)
   ⚠️  Having a Copilot Pro subscription does NOT mean unlimited Codespaces — you can still exhaust
       your 180 core-hour quota. If you hit the limit, increase your spending limit (see below).
   • Monitor your usage at: https://github.com/settings/billing/summary

⚠️  If you see "out of monthly free usage or exceeded your budget for Codespaces":
   This can happen even on a Copilot Pro plan (180 core-hours/month, not unlimited).

   ✅ Quick Fix — do this right now:
   1. Increase your Codespaces spending limit:
      → https://github.com/settings/billing/spending_limit
      Find "Codespaces" → "Edit limit" → set to $5 → "Update limit"
      (You are only charged for usage beyond your free quota, ~$0.18/hr on 2-core)
   2. (Optional) Delete old Codespaces to free up resources:
      → https://github.com/codespaces  → ⋯ → "Delete"
   3. Create a new 2-core Codespace on this repository and wait for setup to finish.

   🔁 Or wait: your included quota resets at the start of each calendar month.

🔄 To restart the exercise from scratch:
   1. Go to https://github.com/settings/billing/summary to check/increase your Codespaces budget.
   2. Delete any old Codespaces at https://github.com/codespaces to free up quota.
   3. Create a new Codespace on the exercise repository using a 2-core machine.
   4. The post-create script will set up MongoDB, Python, and Node.js automatically.
   5. Follow the exercise instructions linked from the repository README.
